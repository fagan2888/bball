Proportion of Shots Made by Left / Right of Net The location from which a shot is attempted is recorded relative to the net's location, which is given as $(0,0)$. Therefore, we can select shots according to side, left or right of the net—left has values less than 0, right greater than or equal to 0. We examine the proportion of shots made by side, and run a simple hypothesis test.

Empirically, we see that $p_L = 0.4099$ and $p_R = 0.4752$. As each shot attempted is an distributed IID with a Bernoulli distribution, we can calculate a 95% confidence interval: \[ CI_L = (0.4071, 0.4127); CI_R = (0.4729, 0.4775) \] These confidence intervals suggest a statistically significant difference in means (at least under a Bayesian interpretation). A hypothesis test supports this: the $Z$-statistic for a difference in means between the two populations—left- and right-hand sides of the net—is equal to 34.9, which is highly significant at all significance levels.

We are therefore able to reject the hypothesis that the proportion of shots made is the same on either side of the net, instead suggesting that a higher proportion of shots are made from the right side than the left side.
